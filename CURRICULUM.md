# CURRICULUM.md

# French Learning Curriculum

### TEF / TCF Canada

### A0 → B2

### Duration: 160 Days

---

# Mission

The objective of this curriculum is not general French fluency.

The objective is:

> Reach a strong B2 level required for TEF / TCF Canada.

Every lesson must contribute directly to success in the exam.

---

# Learning Philosophy

Learning happens every day.

Progress is measured weekly.

Assessment happens monthly.

Consistency is more important than intensity.

---

# Daily Learning Structure

Every learning day follows exactly the same structure.

## Grammar

Learn one concept.

---

## Vocabulary

Learn approximately 20 new words.

Review previous vocabulary using spaced repetition.

---

## Listening

10–20 minutes.

Use level-appropriate resources.

---

## Speaking

Daily conversation with the AI teacher.

No exceptions.

---

## Reading

Read one short text.

---

## Writing

Short writing assignment.

---

## Homework

Complete assigned exercises.

---

# Weekly Structure

Monday → Thursday

New material.

Friday

Review.

Saturday

Weekly Quiz.

Sunday

Light review or catch-up.

---

# Monthly Structure

At the end of every month:

* Grammar Review
* Vocabulary Review
* Listening Assessment
* Speaking Assessment
* Writing Assessment
* Mock TEF / TCF Session

# Learning Phases

---

# Phase 1

## Foundation

Duration

Approximately Weeks 1–4

Target Level

A0 → A1

Objectives

* French alphabet
* Pronunciation
* Greetings
* Numbers
* Articles
* Gender
* Present tense
* Essential verbs
* Daily expressions
* Basic conversations

Target Vocabulary

600 words

Books

* Grammaire Progressive A1
* Vocabulaire Progressif A1
* Édito A1

Listening

Coffee Break French

Easy French

Homework Focus

Grammar accuracy.

---

# Phase 2

## Building Communication

Weeks 5–9

Target Level

A1 → A2

Objectives

* Past tense
* Future expressions
* Asking questions
* Shopping
* Directions
* Restaurant
* Travel
* Family
* Work

Vocabulary Goal

1200 words

Listening

InnerFrench (easy episodes)

French Comprehensible Input

Writing

Simple paragraphs.

---

# Phase 3

## Functional Independence

Weeks 10–16

Target Level

A2 → B1

Objectives

* More complex grammar
* Longer conversations
* Reading authentic texts
* Listening comprehension
* Opinion expression
* Argument building

Vocabulary Goal

2000 words

Writing

150–200 words.

Speaking

Role-play every day.

---

# Phase 4

## Exam Preparation

Weeks 17–23

Target Level

B1 → B2

Objectives

Everything is exam-oriented.

Focus on

* Listening speed
* Speaking fluency
* Writing structure
* Reading strategies
* Time management

Vocabulary Goal

3000+ words

Books

Official TEF / TCF preparation books.

Listening

RFI

TV5 Monde

France Inter

# Weekly Goals

Every week should improve:

Grammar

Vocabulary

Listening

Speaking

Reading

Writing

No skill may be ignored.

---

# Assessment Rules

A phase is completed only if the student demonstrates:

Grammar understanding

Vocabulary retention

Listening comprehension

Speaking confidence

Writing quality

Otherwise:

Repeat weak topics before moving forward.

---

# Teacher Rules

The teacher must always:

Read MEMORY.md first.

Follow PROJECT.md.

Never skip prerequisite topics.

Never teach content outside the current phase.

Adapt explanations.

Do not adapt the curriculum.

---

# Resource Strategy

Books provide structure.

Podcasts improve listening.

AI provides interaction.

Homework creates retention.

Weekly reviews create consistency.

---

# Success Criteria

The curriculum succeeds when the student can:

Understand authentic spoken French.

Participate in everyday conversations.

Write structured texts.

Read intermediate French comfortably.

Achieve approximately B2 level for TEF / TCF Canada.

---

# Final Principle

The curriculum is fixed.

Daily lessons may adapt.

The destination never changes.
